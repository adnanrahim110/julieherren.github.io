Women in business:
You laugh or you cry

This poignant and unexpectedly funny coming-of-age story opens our eyes to what is absurd and shocking about a woman’s daily existence in the corporate cubicles and fraught families of modern America.

Get the book now

You’ll recognize our girl

Honey Malone is all business. She always has been. No one has ever told her otherwise. A college honors student, all Honey wants is for her powerful businessman Dad to feel proud of her. She enters the working world with no compunction, starting at the bottom of the totem pole, sharpening the pencils of her OCD first boss, Mr. Meloso (sweet in Portuguese), who is decidedly NOT meloso.

As Honey rises through the ranks and becomes a formidable opponent, the cutthroat world of business (and family) doesn’t leave her unscathed. From sexist remarks to handsy bosses and drunk clients to an adored father who takes a steep fall from grace, Honey lets it all roll off her back— —But she doesn’t forget. And boy does she get her reward. Our girl Honey Malone charges into her future with courage and impatience.

What to expect

The Seven Bosses of Honey Malone rips the white male privilege out of the hands of the bosses who, for better or worse, shape the career of a smart, young, determined woman. Honey Malone is every woman you’ve ever seen interrupted, talked over, underestimated, and undermined by a man in the workplace, as the rest of the women in the room look on in unadulterated rage. She is you. And you. And me. And all of us.

The Seven Bosses of Honey Malone by Juli Herren was a fun, funny and engaging read. The book follows the trials and tribulations of “our girl” Honey Malone from college graduation through seven distinctive bosses and multiple decades, during which Honey learns a multitude of lessons about work, life, love, relationships, betrayal and following your dreams. From her first hilariously OCD boss at a real estate firm to her final (it’s a surprise) boss, Honey grows from a spunky but untried executive assistant to a respected business woman and content creator with a love of life and an inspirational passion for words. As a content creator myself, I’d never before read a novel centered on that profession. I felt so seen as when Honey talked about her time writing for Behemoth Tech’s home page where, though she was viewed as a “subhuman” she found fulfillment in helping create “the stories that made the business come alive.” Honey’s love for language is a theme that spans the book and is evident in Honey’s personal as well as professional life. She keeps track of clever bumper stickers (“When cryptography is outlawed, bayl bhgynjf jvyy unir cevinpl”) and surreptitiously jots her boyfriend’s frequent and hilarious malapropisms on dinner napkins to remember them (“She wasn’t dead. She was just unconscientious.”) Though the book is ostensibly about Honey’s bosses, in reality Honey’s bosses serve more as pins in the map of Honey’s rambling life journey, as she navigates familial relationships, friendships, multiple suitors, and more. The journey is interesting and at times both funny and poignant, making The Seven Bosses of Honey Malone a pleasant and worthwhile read.
